# For the Love of Food: Cooking, Memory and Culture

This project explores how food functions as a site of memory, identity, and cultural expression. Through a series of interviews and recipes, it examines how cooking practices reflect lived experience, community, and the transmission of knowledge across generations.

## Project Overview

This website was created as part of a CTS Capstone Project at the University of Guelph. It combines oral history, food studies, and digital storytelling to explore the cultural meanings embedded in everyday cooking.

The project is based around three main components:

- **Interviews** — Conversations with individuals about their experiences with food, memory, and cultural identity  
- **Recipes** — Dishes connected to each interview, presented as both instructions and cultural artifacts  
- **Research** — Theoretical frameworks from anthropology, sociology and food studies used to analyze the interviews  

## Key Themes

- Food as a form of cultural memory  
- Recipes as practices of knowledge transmission  
- The relationship between food, place, and environment  
- Migration and the transformation of culinary traditions  
- Cooking as a social and communal activity
