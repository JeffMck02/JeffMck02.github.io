Context

For Rami, dishes like shish barak are closely tied to memory, family, and tradition. Recipes like this are not simply learned, but passed down across generations—moving from his grandmother, to him, and now shared further. These dishes are shaped not only by ingredients, but by the people who make them and the contexts in which they are prepared.

Shish barak is a traditional dish found across the Levant, consisting of small beef-filled dumplings that are baked and then finished in a warm yogurt sauce. Often associated with home cooking, it requires time, patience, and attention to detail.

The process itself reflects a form of knowledge built through practice. Shaping the dumplings, balancing the yogurt sauce, and managing heat without curdling it are all skills developed over time and shared within families. In this way, cooking becomes a way of preserving both memory and cultural knowledge.

Recipe

Dough

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp milk powder (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup water

Filling

  • 1/2 lb ground beef (or lamb)
  • 3 tbsp pine nuts (toasted)
  • 1 small onion
  • Handful of parsley
  • 3 garlic cloves (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp seven spice or allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Yogurt Sauce

  • 32 oz plain yogurt (not Greek yogurt)
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 cups water (adjust as needed)

Garnish

  • Garlic (to taste)
  • 3 tbsp dried mint
  • Coriander
  • Parsley

Instructions

  1. Dough: Combine dry ingredients, then add oil and water. Mix until a smooth dough forms. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Filling: Toast pine nuts in olive oil until golden. Finely mince garlic, onion, and parsley. Mix with ground meat, spices, salt, and pepper. Cook on medium heat until browned, then add pine nuts.
  3. Dumplings: Roll dough thin and cut into small circles. Fill with meat mixture, fold over, and pinch into a tortellini-like shape. Bake at 400°F for 10–15 minutes until slightly dry and lightly browned.
  4. Yogurt Sauce: Heat yogurt slowly on low heat, adding salt and cornstarch mixture. Stir constantly and do not let it boil, or it will split.
  5. Finish: Add dumplings to the yogurt sauce and cook for a few minutes. Garnish with sautéed garlic, dried mint, parsley, and pine nuts.
