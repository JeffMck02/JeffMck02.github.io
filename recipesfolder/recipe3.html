Context

For Ben, this recipe reflects a style of cooking shaped by experimentation rather than strict tradition. Growing up, cooking for others pushed him to try new combinations and flavors through trial and error, developing an approach that values adaptability and creativity.

Duck chaufa captures this approach clearly. The dish originates from Chinese-Peruvian cuisine (chifa), a fusion that developed through Chinese migration to Peru in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. It combines Chinese cooking techniques with local Peruvian ingredients, creating something that is both hybrid and evolving.

For Ben, soy sauce is not just an ingredient but a tool, something that adds depth, balance, and complexity. In this dish, it reflects both global food movements and his own emphasis on how a simple ingredient can transform a dish.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 litre day-old rice (kept in the coldest, driest part of the fridge)
  • 1 duck leg confit (per portion) (can substitute chicken)
  • 250 ml sweet corn kernels
  • 250 ml edamame
  • 1 large tablespoon ginger, shallot, garlic purée (“triple threat”)
  • 6 oz soy sauce (use a good quality brand)
  • 6 oz sweet chili sauce
  • Cilantro and scallions (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat a flat cooking surface or pan and fry the rice until it begins to develop colour.
  2. In a separate area, cook the duck leg until the fat renders. Chop into small pieces and add to the rice.
  3. Mix 1.5 oz soy sauce with 1.5 oz water. Add to the rice and allow it to cook slightly to deepen the colour and flavor.
  4. Add the “triple threat” (ginger, garlic, shallot) and cook briefly until aromatic (about 30 seconds).
  5. Add the corn and edamame, mixing thoroughly.
  6. Add the remaining soy sauce and water mixture, stirring until evenly coated.
  7. Add one ladle (about 3 oz) of sweet chili sauce and mix well.
  8. Garnish with scallions, cilantro, and chilies before serving.
