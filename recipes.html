About These Recipes

The recipes I included in this project are reflections of the cultural meanings and lived experiences I explored throughout my interviews. As demonstrated in The Anthropologist’s Cookbook, which inspired the format for this section, recipes can function as forms of storytelling, offering us insight into the people, places and traditions that they come from. Instead of staying stuck in place in any one place or time, they are able to carry histories of migration, memory, and everyday practices. This perspective is also explored in Gillian Crowther’s Eating Culture, where food is understood to be part of engagement with nature and culture, in that sense, recipes reflect how individuals interact with their environment, communities, and identities through daily acts of cooking. Across these featured dishes, we see how food not only acts as fuel but also as a way of maintaining relationships, preserving knowledge and expressing one’s culture and identity.
